Key observations:
- Fabric weight, layering, color range, color intensity: near-perfect agreement
- Silhouette: CLIP tends to score slightly more "oversized" than manual, possibly because baggy/relaxed Number (N)ine looks register as oversized in CLIP's training data (which is calibrated to mainstream fashion, not avant-garde)
- Distressing: CLIP picks up a moderate signal even when I scored 0, possibly detecting fringe, washed fabrics, or raw construction as "distressed." The threshold is different between manual binary (3+ looks with rips/tears) and CLIP's continuous score
- These discrepancies are EXPECTED and GOOD for the methodology section: you can discuss how human scoring and automated scoring capture different aspects of the same attributes
