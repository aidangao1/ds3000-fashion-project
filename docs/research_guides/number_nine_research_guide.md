## Where to Find Images

1. **Vogue Runway** - vogue.com/fashion-shows/ - Best source for SS07 onward. Search "Number N Ine" + season.
2. **FirstView** - firstview.com - Has Number Nine collections from SS05 onward, large galleries with 100+ photos per show.
3. **ARCHIVE.pdf** - archivepdf.net/number-nine - Magazine scans, lookbooks, editorial shoots. Great supplementary source.
4. **YouTube runway footage** - Search "Number Nine [season] runway". Videos exist for most seasons from SS02 onward. You can screenshot key looks.
5. **Grailed listings** - grailed.com/designers/number-n-ine - Individual pieces with season tags. Useful for detail shots and confirming attributes.
6. **Pinterest** - Search "Number Nine [collection name] runway". Aggregated archive photos.
7. **SaolMortem / Truss Archive** - Editorial overviews with select runway images.

## Scoring Guide - Number (N)ine Specifics

Number (N)ine's baseline is very different from Yohji. Keep these calibration notes in mind:

### Silhouette (1-5)
Miyashita's silhouettes are generally slimmer than Yohji. His baseline is around 2-3 (fitted to slightly relaxed), with some collections pushing to 4 (oversized layered looks in FW05 "The High Streets," FW09 "A Closed Feeling"). He almost never hits 5 the way Yohji does. Skinny jeans, slim blazers, and fitted leather jackets are core Number (N)ine pieces. Score relative to the universal rubric, not relative to Yohji.

### Fabric Weight (1-5)
Number (N)ine uses a lot of lightweight materials: cotton tees, denim, flannel, lightweight leather. Fall collections skew heavier with wool coats and layered pieces, but rarely hit 5. Spring collections often feel like thrift-store-light: worn cotton, thin knits, gauzy layers.

### Layering Density (1-5)
This is where Number (N)ine often scores higher than you'd expect. Miyashita was known for complex layering: tee under flannel under blazer under overcoat, with a scarf on top. FW05 "The High Streets" is legendary for cramming multiple motifs and materials into single outfits. Score the typical look, not the extremes.

### Color Range (1-5)
Number (N)ine has more color variation season-to-season than Yohji. Some collections are near-monochrome black/gray ("Noir," "A Closed Feeling"), while others incorporate heavy earth tones, reds, plaids, camouflage ("The High Streets," "Give Peace a Chance"). Check each collection individually.

### Color Intensity (1-5)
When Number (N)ine uses color, it tends to be muted/washed-out/vintage-feeling. Think faded flannel reds, dusty olive, sun-bleached denim. Rarely vivid or saturated. Exceptions exist in graphic prints.

### Distressing (0/1)
This is Number (N)ine's signature. Almost every collection features distressing of some kind: bullet-hole tees, frayed hems, ripped denim, shredded edges, cracked leather. Score 1 for most collections. The style of distressing varies: "bullet-hole" perforations (SS04), loose/ragged fraying (FW03), paint splatter, and cigarette-burn-style holes. Note the type in your notes.

### Patchwork (0/1)
Miyashita used patchwork more selectively than Yohji. FW05 "The High Streets" is the iconic patchwork collection (hybrid cargo pants made from multiple fabrics). Some other collections feature it but many don't.

### Raw Edges (0/1)
Common in Number (N)ine but not universal. The deconstructed/grunge collections (FW03 "Touch Me I'm Sick," FW09 "A Closed Feeling") feature raw edges heavily. The more polished collections ("Noir," "About a Boy") tend to have cleaner construction.

### Graphic Prints (0/1)
Almost always 1. Graphic tees are a Number (N)ine staple: band logos (Nirvana, Guns N' Roses flips), skull motifs, text prints, artistic illustrations. Music notes sewn into linings and basting. Even the more tailored collections usually include graphic elements.

## Key Collections to Watch For

These are the most data-rich and analytically interesting collections. Prioritize getting good imagery for these:

**FW05 "The High Streets"** - The most complex collection. Hybrid garments combining Native American elements (fur, fringe, feathers) with grunge (flannel, ripped denim, Cobain sunglasses). The iconic cargo pants are made from 5+ different fabrics. This will likely be an outlier in your data and a great discussion point.

**FW09 "A Closed Feeling"** - The final collection. Inspired by Miyashita's isolation in an Alaskan hotel during depression. Dark palette, embellished masks, wraps, heavily detailed garments. Raw edges throughout. Compare this to Yohji's darker collections for cross-designer clustering.

**FW07 "Love God Murder"** - Named after the Johnny Cash box set. Victorian/gothic elegance with rock undertones. One of the more "mature" collections that shows Miyashita's evolution from pure grunge toward refined darkness.

**FW04 "Give Peace a Chance"** - Heavy distressing (bullet-hole style), peace/protest messaging, military surplus references. Compare to Yohji's military-themed FW26.

**SS07 "About a Boy"** - More youthful, preppy elements mixed with Miyashita's typical darkness. Good contrast piece within the catalog.
